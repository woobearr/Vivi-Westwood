430 Kings Road 1971 – 1980

In the late 1960s, while the hippie movement dominated London's fashion scene, Vivienne Westwood and Malcolm McLaren were drawn to rebellion and 1950s aesthetics. They opened Let it Rock boutique in 1971, specializing in Teddy Boy attire. Quickly evolving, they shifted focus to biker gear, then provocative t-shirts at Too Fast to Live, Too Young to Die. Renamed Sex in 1974, it became a hub for punk culture, later transforming into Seditionaries. Disillusioned by punk's mainstream adoption, they renamed it Worlds End in 1980. Today, it remains unchanged, preserving its original design.

The Early Years 1981 – 1987

In 1984, Nostalgia of Mud (later Worlds End) closes as Vivienne Westwood moves to Italy. Her 'Hypnos' collection in 1984 gains acclaim at Hanae Mori's Tokyo showcase. Another London boutique opens on Davies Street in 1988. The iconic orb logo debuts in 1986, symbolizing tradition and the future. Carlo D’Amario takes the helm as Managing Director of Vivienne Westwood Ltd. The 1981 'Pirate' Collection, co-created with Malcolm McLaren, influences the brand's aesthetic with romantic, historically inspired designs. Westwood's innovative garment construction blends historical cuts with dynamic, body-conscious designs.
